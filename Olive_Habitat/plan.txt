This study proposes an AI-based early-warning framework for detecting climate-induced water stress in olive orchards of the Ghafsai region near Fez, using multi-source satellite and climate data processed in Google Earth Engine (GEE).

Results show that the proposed model achieves strong predictive performance (RÂ² > 0.70), with rainfall deficits, temperature anomalies, and soil moisture identified as the dominant drivers of olive water stress. Spatially explicit risk maps reveal recurrent stress hotspots during late spring and summer, highlighting periods of heightened vulnerability. ðŸ”¹ Abstract â€“ Article 2
Assessing Climate-Driven Habitat Suitability and Future Decline Risk of Wild Olive Ecosystems in Northern Morocco Using AI-Based Species Distribution Modeling

Native Mediterranean tree species are increasingly threatened by climate change, yet their future habitat dynamics remain poorly quantified in North Africa. In Morocco, research on climate-driven biodiversity impacts has largely focused on a limited number of emblematic species, leaving wild olive (Olea europaea var. sylvestris) ecosystems underexplored despite their ecological and socio-economic importance. This study assesses the current and future habitat suitability of wild olive ecosystems in the Ghafsai region near Fez using AI-based species distribution modeling.

Occurrence records were combined with bioclimatic variables, topographic factors, and land-cover data to train a Random Forest / MaxEnt model that estimates habitat suitability under present climatic conditions and future climate scenarios (RCP 4.5 and RCP 8.5). Model performance was evaluated using AUC and threshold-dependent metrics, and spatial projections were generated to identify areas of habitat contraction, expansion, and climatic refugia.

The results indicate a significant reduction in suitable habitats under future climate scenarios, particularly at lower elevations and in areas experiencing increased temperature and reduced precipitation. However, several high-elevation and north-facing zones emerge as potential climate refugia, offering priority targets for conservation and restoration strategies. This study provides one of the first AI-driven assessments of wild olive climate vulnerability in northern Morocco and highlights the value of integrating machine learning with climate projections to support biodiversity conservation under climate change. The proposed framework is transferable to other native tree species and regions across the Mediterranean basin.
